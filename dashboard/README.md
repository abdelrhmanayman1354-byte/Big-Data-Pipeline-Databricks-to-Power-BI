## 🌐 2. The Big Picture: Overview & Performance
First, we look at the high-level health of the business to understand the scale of operations.

![Overview Page](photo/Overview.png)

Upon opening the dashboard, we see a powerful image: The restaurant has achieved **2.90 Billion in Revenue**, with **11 Million Orders** and 35 Million items sold. These figures confirm a high-volume business operating with significant efficiency. However, at the same time, the average rating of **3.70** raises a slight question mark: Is the customer experience as strong as the financial numbers?

Additionally, the revenue distribution shows that **Cash** accounts for 50% of transactions, and **Dine-in** represents about 40%. This indicates a traditional business model with heavy reliance on physical presence and less on digital channels.

---

## 📈 3. Revenue Dynamics: Sales Analysis
Now we dig into the "Flow" of money. How consistent is our growth, and what are the seasonal patterns?

![Sales Page](photo/Sales.png)

Looking at the Month-over-Month (**MoM**) change, we find that revenue is up by **+2.6%**, which is a positive growth indicator. Conversely, the Average Order Value (**AOV**) has dropped by **-1%**. This means customers are ordering more frequently but spending less per order—a trend that could impact profitability over time.

We also notice that performance is inconsistent. There is clear growth in certain months like **+6.32% in March** and **+5.43% in May**, but significant drops in others, such as **-5.45% in February** and **-5.58% in September**. This volatility suggests the business is highly susceptible to seasonal factors or changing customer behavior.

## 🍔 4. Menu Intelligence: Product Analysis
Is our menu balanced? We differentiate between the "Stars" and the "Underperformers" to optimize the kitchen's output.

![Products Page](photo/Products.png)

A product like **Kebab** is the undisputed leader, generating about 505 Million, while other items like Tajines and Chicken compete strongly. However, deep diving reveals a gap between:
*   **Best Sellers** (Highest Volume)
*   **Top Earners** (Highest Revenue)
*   **Top Rated** (Highest Satisfaction)
This misalignment suggests that some high-resource items may not be delivering maximum value, necessitating a strategic menu review.

## 👥 5. The Human Element: Customer Insights
Numbers don't order food, people do. This page identifies who our customers are and how we can retain them.

![Customer Page](photo/Customer.png)

The customer base has reached **200,000**, with a **Retention Rate of 54.56%**. While acceptable, there is room for improvement. 
Analyzing the segments reveals a critical challenge:
*   **48.38% Loyal** | **24.49% Slipping** | **11.33% At Risk** | **9.94% Lost**
Nearly **45% of our customers** are in a danger zone (Slipping or At Risk). More concerningly, the **Reorder Behavior** chart shows that the "Lost" segment was highly active before disappearing. We are not just losing customers; we are losing our most engaged ones.

---

## ⏱️ 6. The Heartbeat: Time Analysis
Efficiency is found in the seconds. When is the kitchen under pressure, and when is it idle?

![Time Page](photo/Time.png)

The peak performance day is **Sunday**, with the peak hour at **20:00 (8 PM)**. The average hourly orders stand at **5.05K** , which is an excellent indicator of increasing activity.
However, the weekly distribution reveals a gap: Weekends contribute **33.3%** compared to **66.7%** during the week. While the workweek contributes more in total, the intensity is concentrated in specific "Golden Hours," leaving many periods underutilized.

---

## 🧠 Executive Summary & Conclusion
The story tells us that the restaurant is successful and powerful in terms of volume and growth. However, clear red flags exist: **Declining AOV**, **monthly sales volatility**, a high percentage of **customers at risk**, and **unbalanced time utilization**. 

**In short: The business is growing... but it is not yet operating at its maximum potential efficiency.**

## 🚀 Strategic Recommendations
1.  **Retention Focus:** Target "Slipping" and "At Risk" segments with personalized offers and loyalty programs.
2.  **AOV Boost:** Implement Bundles and Upselling strategies, especially during peak hours.
3.  **Digital Shift:** Reduce reliance on Cash and Dine-in by boosting Digital Channels (Delivery / Online).
4.  **Menu Audit:** Realign the menu to focus on high-profit items rather than just high-volume ones.
5.  **Low-Time Optimization:** Introduce "Happy Hour" or special offers on **Tuesdays** and early weekdays.
6.  **Experience Enhancement:** Aim to raise the average rating from **3.7 → 4.0+** by improving service quality and consistency.
